310.41 Healthy Horizons Applicant/Recipient Groups

Each individual (except for spouses) is a separate applicant/recipient group. Spouses who are both eligible for HH are a two-member applicant/recipient group.

Example: Chris J., age 65, and his brother, Bill, age 67, are applying for HH. Each is a separate applicant/recipient group. The income and resources of each brother has no effect on the eligibility of the other.

A household may have one or more HH applicant/recipient groups and an NMP, MNO, or HB applicant/recipient group.

310.43 NMP Applicant/Recipient Groups

Separate applicant/recipient groups are set up for SSI-related and TANF-related or GA-related benefits.

Reminder: Eligibility must be considered for NMP for the Family (NMPF) as a single budget group before determining the eligibility of one individual.

  • SSI-related individual.

Each individual, other than spouses, is a separate applicant/recipient group. Spouses who are both eligible for an SSI-related category are one applicant/recipient group.

The CAO will not count the income and resources of the PA, PJ, or PM recipient or of an applicant who is eligible but not yet approved when determining eligibility of other household members for NMP or MNO.

A child of an SSI-related NMP applicant/recipient is not included in the eligibility determination, whether or not the child is applying for or receiving MA.

The spouse of an SSI-related NMP applicant/recipient is included in the eligibility determination if the spouse is not elderly or disabled and is not applying for or receiving MA. The income of the spouse who is not elderly or disabled is deemed if it is more than 50 percent of the federal benefit rate for SSI. (See Chapter 355, Deeming Income and Resources.)

  • TANF-related or GA-related individual.

Members of an immediate family who are applying for a TANF-related or GA-related category are included in each applicant/recipient group as either an eligible member or a counted member.

Relatives who are not immediate family members may be included or may be a separate applicant/recipient group. Unrelated individuals will always be a separate applicant/recipient group.

The spouse of the NMP applicant/recipient who does not receive or is not eligible for any other coverage group of MA is included in the eligibility decision for NMP. The income of the spouse is deemed. (See Chapter 355, Deeming Income and Resources.) Â Â Â Â
