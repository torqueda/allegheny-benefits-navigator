Disability Verified by SSA or MRT

Scenario 1

The applicant is a single 25-year old, permanently disabled, the disability is MRT certified. Earned income is $1000 a month, and there are no resources reported on the application.

The CAO Action: The CAO authorizes PH 00 M since the applicant meets the requirements of the SSI-related Healthy Horizons program and completes a DAP referral as an application for Social Security benefits is not required, but the pursuit of SSA benefits is beneficial to the individual.

Scenario 2

The CAO Action: The CAO authorizes MG 90 D since the applicant is not eligible in Healthy Horizons due to excess resources. The CAO completes a DAP referral as an application for Social Security benefits is not required, but the pursuit of SSA benefits is beneficial to the individual.

Scenario 3

The applicant is a single 60-year old, permanently disabled, receiving RSDI due to disability, RSDI is $950 a month, not receiving Medicare, and the verification of reported resources was not provided with the application.

The CAO Action: The CAO authorizes MG 90 D and pends for verification of resources.

Scenario 3.1

The individual provides the verification of resources. Resources are under the Healthy Horizons limit.

The CAO Action: The CAO enters verified resources information in eCIS and runs eligibility. The CAO authorizes PH 00 M.

The CAO Action: If the individual indicates he/she is no longer disabled, the CAO will accept that personâs statement and maintain the eligibility in a MAGI category (if they meet other eligibility requirements).Â Â If the individual fails to cooperate and still maintains they are disabled, the CAO will maintain eligibility in a MAGI category and can choose to refer the individual to MRT so they can be evaluated for a disability-related MA category.

NOTE: The CAO must make the referral to MRT if the individual is not eligible for a non-disability related MA category, such as MAGI.

Scenario 7.3

The individual returns income verification and the PA 1663 which shows that the individual is disabled for less than 12 months.

The CAO Action: The CAO authorizes MG 91 as the individual is considered to be a Parent/Caretaker age 21-64 with income at or between 33%-133% FPL. The individual will remain eligible for MG 91until circumstances change.

Scenarios for Women Who Report a Pregnancy

Scenario 15

The recipient of MG 91calls the CAO to report a pregnancy.

The CAO Action: The CAO updates the pregnancy screen and authorizes MG 91 P. The individual stays eligible in MG 91 P until the end of the 12-month postpartum period.

Scenario 16

The recipient of MG 90 D calls the CAO to report a pregnancy.

The CAO Action: The CAO updates the pregnancy screen and authorizes MG 90 P. The individual stays eligible in MG 90 P until the end of the 12-month postpartum period.
