522.333 Counting Sponsor Resources and Income

If the sponsor lives with his or her spouse, the CAO must count the spouse's income and resources, even if they were married after the sponsor signed the sponsorship agreement.

Â Â  7 CFR Â§ 273.4(c)(2)

Figure out the total countable resources of the sponsor and spouse as follows:

1. Â Add the resources of the sponsor and his or her spouse.

2. Â Deduct $1,500.

3. Â Assume the amount left is available to the noncitizen.

Figure out the income deemed to the noncitizen's household as follows:

1. Â Deduct the 20% earned income deduction from the combined countable earnings of the sponsor and spouse.

2. Â Combine the amount left with any countable unearned income of the sponsor and spouse.

NOTE: The sponsor is entitled to standard SNAP earned and unearned income exclusions. See 550.5, Income Excluded in Determining Eligibility.

3. Â Figure out household size by counting the number of individuals who the sponsor claims or could claim as a dependent for Federal income tax purposes.

4. Â Get the monthly gross income limit based on household size from Chapter 568, Appendix A.

5. Â Subtract the monthly gross income limit from Step 4 from the sponsorâs income from Step 2. Â Â The amount left is considered unearned income.

6. Â Deem the amount left from Step 5 to the non-citizen's household as unearned income regardless of whether the non-citizen gets the money from the sponsor.Â
