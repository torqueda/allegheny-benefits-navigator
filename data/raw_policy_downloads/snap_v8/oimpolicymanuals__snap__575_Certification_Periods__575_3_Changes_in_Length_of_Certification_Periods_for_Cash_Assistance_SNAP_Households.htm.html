575.3 Shortening theÂ Certification Periods for SNAP Households

When a change in circumstances requires cash assistance to stop and the CAO does not have enough information to determine the SNAP benefit amount, the CAO must take the following actions:

  • Send a request for clarification or proof.

  • If the household fails to respond to the request, send the household an Advance Notice of Adverse Action advising the household of its plans to terminate the SNAP benefits.

The CAO must continue SNAP benefits during the appeal process if the household asks for a hearing on the proposed plan to stop cash assistance benefits.

575.31 Â Budget Alignment

The CAO must align all budgets that can be aligned at the next application, SAR review, renewal or reported change that results in an eligibility determination. See Appendix C for general guidelines to follow when aligning budgets within a case record.

NOTE: The renewal year for a 36-month ESAP budget will never align with the renewal year of a 12-month MA or TANF budget, however the CAO should still align the renewal month so the clientÂ  recipient receives their notices for all budgets at the same time (the ESAP interim contact letter PA1937 and the MA/TANF renewal packets). If the recipient fails to complete their next MA/TANF renewal, those budgets can close and the recipient will still have the remaining two years of their three-year ESAP certification, thus getting their full certification period.

When a SNAP household enrolled in SAR applies for Cash or Medical Assistance (MA) using an application form (PA 600 or PA 600R) that includes SNAP, the CAO must align the renewal dates.

Exception: Alignment policy does not apply to Extended SNAP (FS 43) Â households. Â 

NOTE: Remember that MA may be opened with a renewal period of less than 12 months. SNAP certification periods may not always have to be shortened to align renewal periods for all budgets.

The CAO may shorten the SNAP certification period by completing a renewal interview. If the household does not cooperate in providing the necessary information to complete the renewal, the CAO must close the budget.

NOTE: If the SNAP packet was mailed and not returned, the CAO will close SNAP using reason code 042

After the renewal, the CAO must:

  • Assign a new certification period that aligns with the Cash or MA renewal period.

  • Send a confirming notice to the household stating the benefit amount (even if it has not changed) and new certification period. Â

The CAO is not required to issue supplemental benefits or submit an overpayment referral after completing the renewal if:

  • The individual does not submit verification of required information within 30 days but does provide it within 60 days

  • The CAO does not process the Cash or MA application timely.

If a household files an appeal within 15 days of the date the Confirming Notice is mailed because the CAO proposes to decrease SNAP benefits and elects to continue to receive the higher benefit amount, the CAO must continue the original benefit amount until the hearing decision or the certification period ends, whichever is earlier. The budgets cannot be aligned.

If the household appeals after 15 days but before 90 days, benefits continue in the reduced amount until the hearing decision. Budgets can be aligned.

The CAO must narrate all eligibility decisions and case actions made at application, SAR, renewal, reported change and verbal or written contact with the household. For renewals, the CAP must narrate how the renewal was conducted (face-to-face, mail, telephone). Case comments must also include the statement that data exchanges (DX) were reviewed for all individuals who may affect the households eligibility.

575.32 Â System Process for Shortening the SNAP Certification Period

When opening a Cash Assistance or MA budget, the CAO must:

  • Process the case as a Program Add and be sure to select the appropriate method of verification for each item,

  • Enter all mandatory (*) fields on any additional screens that appear,

  • Update any necessary Individual Non-financial, Resource or Income information until the Execute Eligibility screen is presented. Â Click "Run Eligibility" button, and proceed through the subsequent screens,

  • At the SNAP Budget Authorization Screen, amend the SAR Due Date and the Reapp Due Date fields,

  • At the Post Authorization/Client Notice screen, ensure or change the FS00 notice to Notice 001, option 6,

  • Continue processing through the case and complete all mandatory (*) fields. Submit the case,

  • If the existing SNAP budget does not change as a result of the addition of the Cash or Medical Assistance budgets, the system will not issue Notice 001. Issue a manual notice to inform the household of the revised certification period,

  • Ensure comments are added to the case narrate to reflect the changed dates.

575.33 Â System Process for Updating a Renewal or SAR

  • On the Case Summary Screen, select "Maintenance" from the "What would you like to do today?" drop down and click the "Next" button.

  • On the Left-Hand Navigation bar, click on "Execute Eligibility",

  • Click "Run Eligibility",

  • After eligibility has run, click the "Next" button,

  • The next screen will be the "Eligibility Results" screen. Ensure the benefits are correct and then click the "Next" button.

For a SAR date change:

  • On the "Budget Authorization" screen, click on the calendar or manually type in the SAR Due Date under SAR Due Date, then click on the "Next" button,

For a Reapplication Date change:

  • Click on the calendar or manually type in the Reapplication Due Date under Reapp due Date and then click on the "Next" button,

  • Continue through Case Processing, entering all relevant information to the case and completing all mandatory (*) fields through case commit.
