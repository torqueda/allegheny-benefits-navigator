523.3 Verification

The household must provide proof of Pennsylvania residence. The CAO must accept any verification which reasonably establishes residency and must not require the household to provide any specific document. A household is not required to present a separate document verifying residency if proof is shown on any other required verification.

Acceptable forms of verification are (but not limited to):

  • rent or mortgage statement showing address;

  • utility bill showing address;

  • personal identification showing address;

  • any other document showing name and address

  • collateral contact, if no other documentary proof is available.

R easonable efforts must be made to verify Pennsylvania residency.

Verification is not required for unusual cases where proof of residence may be difficult to prove, such as:

  • Households newly arrived in the county

  • Migrant or seasonal farm workers

  • Homeless persons
