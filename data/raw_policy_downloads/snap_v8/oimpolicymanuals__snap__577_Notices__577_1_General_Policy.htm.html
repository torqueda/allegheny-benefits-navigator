577.1 General Policy

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) On-Demand Language on the PA 253, PFS20792577 (Published February 23, 2022)

Adverse Action and Advance notice for SNAP recipients, PFS18931577 (Published July 11, 2018)

The CAO must give every applicant and recipient household a written notice whenever it takes any action regarding eligibility or benefit amount.

All notices must include, in language the household understands, the following:

  • The reason for the action

  • The Public Law, 7 CFR, or 55 Pa. Code citations supporting the action, shown as P.L. xxx-xxx, 7 CFR 27x.xxx, or 55 Pa. Code 501.xx (The CAO must not use a SNAP Handbook section number as a citation.)

  • The householdâs right to appeal any action taken by asking for a fair hearing

  • The telephone number of the SNAP office

  • The name, address, and telephone number of any person or organization that provides free legal services

The CAO must keep a copy of the paper or electronic notice for three years from the date it was sent.

Manual PA/FS 162 notices must include the mailing date, which is the first business day following the processing of the case record.
