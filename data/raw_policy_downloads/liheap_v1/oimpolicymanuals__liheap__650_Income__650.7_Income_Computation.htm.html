650.72 Representative Income

Representative income is the most accurate picture of the amount of income a household receives.

If the applicant provides partial income documentation from the month of application or the previous month and says this income is representative, the CAOĂ‚Â may use this income to determine whether the household qualifies for LIHEAP. Proof of income may be one pay stub as long as the applicant says it is representative of weekly or bi-weekly pay.

They say this is representative of their income. The CAO uses $450 weekly to determine eligibility. The CAO compares Margaret's annual income of $21,600 to the income limit for three people ($28,635). To determine a representative pay the CAO would divide the YTD income by the number of pays received. This representative pay would then be entered as a projected pay with the frequency the pay is received.
