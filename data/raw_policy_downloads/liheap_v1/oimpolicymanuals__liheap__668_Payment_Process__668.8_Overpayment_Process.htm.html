668.82 Determination of Overpayment

DHS is responsible for identifying overpayments and getting back incorrectly paid LIHEAP funds. The CAO must take the following actions:

  • Decide whether there was an overpayment.

  • Determine the cause of the overpayment.

  • Collect proof of the cause of the overpayment.

  • Recommend what should be done about the overpayment.

  • Give the Office State of Inspector General (OSIG) any information that might affect the action on the overpayment.

DHS will not seek restitution of any overpayment of less than $25 that occurs because of fraud, error or misrepresentation by either the client or the vendor, but a referral shall be made to the Office of State Inspector General.

668.822 Vendor Overpayments

If there is an overpayment because of vendor error, misrepresentation or fraud, DHS must take steps, if necessary, to get the money back. An (OSIG-189)Â should not be filled out for vendor overpayments. Vendors who receive LIHEAP money for which they are ineligible can contact the Vendor Hotline for instructions on how to return the money.

Any benefit that would have been issued to a vendor who is removed from the participating vendors list must be sent to the client's new vendor or directly to the client in accordance with the LIHEAP State Plan.

If a vendor is removed from active participation in LIHEAP, the vendor cannot be reactivated unless authorization is given by the Vendor Unit in Harrisburg.

The Bureau of Program Support is responsible for letting CAOs know about all removals and reinstatements of vendors.

If an overpayment occurs that was not caused by vendor fraud, error or misrepresentation, the overpayment must be considered an administrative error, and restitution must not be required. The vendor must not be held responsible for repayment of administrative errors, and no referral should be made to the OSIG.

The OSIG makes all contacts and performs all collection activities. However, the CAO may accept payments from people who receive benefits, except when the OSIG is considering prosecution. If payment is accepted, the CAO must give a receipt to the person making the payment and send the payment to the OSIG.
