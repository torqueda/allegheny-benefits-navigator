668.4 Types of Payments

Approvals and rejections of applications and regular payments are handled through e-CIS. In addition, underpayments, reissues, second pays and extraordinary pays are processed through the FM module in e-CIS.

668.41 Regular Payment

A regular payment is an application processed through the Case Processing section of e-CIS. The regular payment is the initial approved benefit payment processed through LIHEAP.

There are circumstances when additional checks, aside from the initial regular payment, must be issued to a clientâs vendor. The additional checks may be for partial benefits or for the total amount of the initial benefit. These additional payments are paid through e-CIS.

668.42 Underpayment

An underpayment is processed when a client is not paid enough.

The CAO must process underpayments.

After a payment is made, if the CAO finds an underpayment, it must review the LIHEAP application (HSEA-1). If the worker finds an error on the application, they should refer the matter to a supervisor.

A supervisor must approve all underpayments processed in e-CIS. Underpayments are to be carefully narrated in the record.

NOTE: Underpayments are processed for the current program year only.

668.43 Extraordinary Pays

An extraordinary pay is issued when normal payment processing is unavailable, such as after the regular system payment process is shut down for federal reporting purposes.

Each extraordinary payment is processed for a specific client case and vendor. An additional check is issued through e-CIS to a vendor on behalf of a client. An extraordinary payment can be processed only after an initial payment has been issued.

Supporting documentation for the extraordinary payment must be scanned in the case record and narrated. The Vendor Unit in Harrisburg must process extraordinary payments.

668.44 Reissue

A benefit can be reissued, either in part or in full, because the original payment needs to be sent to a different vendor and the original vendor returned the money to DHS. A reissuance is processed by Â LIHEAP Headquarters in Harrisburg after DHS gets the money back from the vendor.

DHS will reissue a vendor refund, as applicable, for the current or previous program year if:

  • The whereabouts of the household are known,

  • The household continues to reside in Pennsylvania, and

  • The household continues to have a home-heating responsibility.

668.45 Second Pay

A second pay is used to issue the original Cash or Crisis payment in full or in part to a different vendor when none of the original payment has been returned to DHS. A second pay is processed before a refund from the original vendor is received.

After a second pay has been submitted, e-CIS automatically creates an accounts receivable (AR) record attached to a client payment and sends a dunning letter to the vendor who received the original LIHEAP grant, indicating the demographics from the original payment voucher and the amount to be refunded.

Second pays are processed at LIHEAP headquarters in Harrisburg.

NOTE: If a Cash grant is sent to the incorrect vendor, as an administrative error, the entire grant will be refunded to DHS and issued to the correct vendor.
