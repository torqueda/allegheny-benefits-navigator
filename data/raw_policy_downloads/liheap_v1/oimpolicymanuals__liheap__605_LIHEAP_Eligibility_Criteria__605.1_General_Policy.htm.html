605.11 Income Limit

Federal law requires states to set annual income limits for LIHEAP eligibility based on household size. Federal law says the LIHEAP income limit must be at least 110 percent of the poverty level set by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. The income limit also must be no more than either 150 percent of the Federal Poverty Income Guidelines or 60 percent of the state median income, whichever is higher. The limits can be changed each year and are published in the current LIHEAP State Plan. Current income limits are also published in Chapter 650, Appendix A. Ă‚Â Ă‚Â Ă‚Â Ă‚Â

605.15 Applicant Household Composition

A person or a group of people, including related roomers, who are living together in the same unit or structure as one economic unit that pays for its home-heating energy, either directly to an energy provider or indirectly as an undesignated part of rent, can qualify for LIHEAP if they are otherwise eligible.
