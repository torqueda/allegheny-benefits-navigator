# 568.3 Six-Month SAR Review and Renewal

When a household submits a SAR form, the CAO must prospectively determine eligibility for the first month of the new SAR period. If the income is more representative in the second month because of changes reported on the SAR form, the CAO may be required to prospectively determine eligibility for the second month of the SAR period.

When a household submits a renewal form, the CAO must prospectively determine eligibility for the first two months of the new certification period .

## 568.31 Budgeting at the Six-Month SAR Review and at Renewal

### 568.311 At the SAR review

The CAO must prospectively determine eligibility and benefit amount by anticipating income, expenses, and circumstances for the first month of the new SAR period.

- If the household is ineligible the first month and there are no anticipated changes in income, expenses, or circumstances, the CAO must send a Confirming Notice explaining the decision.

- If the household is ineligible the first month but eligible the second month, the CAO must not issue benefits for the first month but must issue benefits for the second month.

- If the household is eligible the first month but is ineligible the second and later months, the CAO must issue benefits for the first month. The CAO must send an Advance Notice of Adverse Action and close SNAP benefits for the second month.

### 568.312 At renewal

The CAO must prospectively determine both eligibility and benefit amount by anticipating income, expenses, and circumstances for the first month of the new certification period.

- If the household is ineligible the first month, the CAO must determine eligibility for the second month. If the household is ineligible for both months, the CAO must not recertify the case and must send a Notice of Ineligibility explaining the decision.

- If the household is ineligible the first month but eligible the second month, the new certification period begins on the first day of the second month.
