# 310.5 Determining Eligibility for Multiple Applicant/Recipient Groups

## 310.51 Healthy Horizons

For a household of Healthy Horizons (HH) applicants/recipients and individuals who are applying for or receiving other MA coverage, the CAO will do the following:

1. Determine income Â eligibility of the HH applicant / recipient according to Chapter 319, Healthy Horizons, Section 319.8, Determining Â Eligibility .

- ComputeÂ the countable income of the family members according to Chapter 319.6, Income .

- Combine the countable gross earned and unearned income of the family members with the countable gross earned and unearned income of the HH applicant/recipients. Apply the deductions allowed under Chapter 319, Healthy Horizons, Section 319.7, Income Deductions .

NOTE: Â An HH applicant who also meets the eligibility requirements for the HB program is included in the eligibility determination for the other household members who are applying for or receiving MA, depending on which income group benefits all the individuals in the household the most.

An applicant/recipient who meets eligibility requirements for both HB and HH must choose one coverage group when deciding whether other family members are eligible. If the applicant/recipient chooses HH, the HH client is not included in the eligibility determination of the remaining members. If the applicant/recipient chooses HB, the HB client is treated according to the rules for the right coverage group for the other members.

2. Determine whether the HH applicant/recipient and spouse are eligible based on their resources according to Chapter 319,

NOTE: Â There is no consideration of resources for individuals in an HH applicant/recipient group if:

- The individual is a child under age 21.

- The HH applicant/recipient is living in the household with a spouse under age 21.

- The HH applicant/recipient is living with an adopted or natural child under age 21.

- The HH applicant/recipient is living with a stepchild under age 21.

- The HH applicant/recipient is living with, taking care of, and in charge of a child under 21.

3. Determine whether the other individuals who are applying are eligible based on their income as follows:

- Compute income eligibility according to the rules for the coverage group.

- Do not include individuals who receive HH or are eligible but not yet approved for HH, in the income and resource eligibility determination of the other individuals who are applying for NMP or MNO .

Exception: For the remaining individuals in the household who are applying for or receiving HB, the individual who receives HH or is eligible for but not yet approved for HH, may be or must be included in the eligibility determination Â for HB. ## 310.52 Healthy Beginnings

For a household of Healthy Beginnings (HB) applicants/recipients and individuals who are applying for or receiving other MA coverage, the CAO will do the following:

1. Determine whether the HB applicant/recipient and other family members who are included in the income count are eligible based on income. (See Chapter 312, Affordable Care Act/Modified Adjusted Gross Income - MAGI ) .

2. Determine whether the other people who are applying for or receiving MA are eligible based on income.

- Compute income eligibility according to the income-eligibility requirements for the right coverage group of MA.

- Pregnant women and qualified HB children related by blood or marriage must be included in the same income-eligibility group for HB.

- An individual who (a) has HB or is eligible but not yet approved for HB and (b) is a legally responsible relative (LRR) spouse or parent) to someone applying for another NMP or MNO coverage group of MA must be included in the income-eligibility determination for the applicant/recipient group. The individual to whom the HB individual is an LRR must also be included.

- Children who receive HB or are eligible but not yet approved for HB, may be included in the income eligibility of the applicant/recipient groups for NMP and MNO, including their parentsâ groups.

- The income of the HB applicant/recipient who is included with the other individuals in the household applying for or receiving MA is treated according to the rules in Chapter 360, NMP Deductions and Chapter 361, MNO Deductions .

- If an HB applicant/recipient is included in the income-eligibility determination for an applicant/recipient group for MA, the income limit is increased by the number of HB applicants/recipients, including any unborn child.

NOTE: Â The HB applicant who also meets the eligibility requirements for the HH program is included in the eligibility determination for the remaining members in the household applying for or receiving MA, according to whichever is most beneficial to all members. The applicant/recipient who meets eligibility requirements for both HB and HH must choose one coverage group when determining eligibility for other family members.

Â If the applicant/recipient chooses HH, the HH individual is not included in the eligibility determination for the other members. If the applicant/recipient chooses HB, the HB individual is treated according to the requirements of the program that the other family members are applying for or receiving.

## 310.54 MNO

For a household of MNO applicants/recipients and family members who are applying for or receiving other MA coverage, the CAO will do the following:

1. Determine income eligibility of the individuals who are applying for or with SSI-related, TANF-related, or GA-related MNO and are related by blood or marriage as if they are one applicant/recipient group, according to Chapter 369, Determining Eligibility for MNO .

NOTE: Â Do not include individuals receiving HH, or eligible but not yet approved for HH, Â as family members, and do not deem their income

NOTE: Â All immediate family members who are applying for or receiving MNO must be treated as if they are one applicant/recipient group. The only exception to this is step relationships.

- Individuals who (a) receive HB, or are eligible but not yet approved for HB, Â and who (b) are legally responsible relatives (such as a spouse or parent) to someone who is applying for MNO must be included in the income eligibility determination.

- Children who receive HB or are eligible but not yet approved for HB, may be included in theÂ parentâs income eligibility determination for MNO.

- The countable income of someone receiving HB who is included with the other family members applying for or receiving MA is treated according to Chapter 361, MNO Deductions and Chapter 369, Determining on Eligibility for MNO .

- If someone who receives HB is included in the income-eligibility decision for an applicant/recipient group for MNO, the income limit is increased, depending on how many HB people are included. Unborn children are also included.

The CAO will not count the resources ofÂ individuals in an applicant/recipient group if the individual is:

- A child under age 21.

- Living in the household with a spouse under age 21.

- Living in the household with an adopted or natural child under age 21.

- Living in the household with a stepchild under age 21.

- Living with, taking care of, and in charge of a child under age 21.

- Deem income and resources of the nonapplicant/nonrecipient LRR according to Chapter 355, Deeming Income and Resources .

NOTE: Â When deeming the income of an individual who is an LRR to both SSI-related and TANF-related or GA-related MNO applicants/recipients in the same group, the CAO treats the nonapplicant member as an SSI-related LRR. There is no deduction given for a dependent of the LRR who is included in the applicant/recipient group for MNO.

Â If the LRR's countable income after allowable deductions is more than 50 percent of the federal benefit rate, the LRR is treated as an SSI-related applicant/recipient. The highest income allowed for the applicant/recipient group for MNO is increased by one individual to include the LRR. The LRR is not approved for MNO.

NOTE: Â When deeming the income of an NA member who is an LRR to both TANF-related and GA-related individuals in the same applicant/recipient group, the CAO treats the NA member as a TANF-related LRR.
