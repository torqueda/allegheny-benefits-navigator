## 368.81 Additions to the Applicant/Recipient Group

When a client asks to add another individual or the CAO says that an individual who is applying for NMP must be included in an applicant/recipient group that already exists, the CAO will treat the individual as an applicant. (See Chapter 304, Application .)

The CAO will take the following actions:

- Ask the client to submit an application form to add the new individual. Mail the application form to the client on the day the request is received. Also, refer the client to COMPASS.

Exception: Â An application form is not needed to add a newborn or to add a child who is coming back into the household from substitute care or another household.

- If the new member has income, determine the groupâs NMP eligibility with the member's countable income included. (See Section 368.3, Continuing NMP , and Section 368.4, NMP Spend-Down . )

- If adding another member would make the group ineligible, do not approve NMP for the applicant. Continue NMP for the existing applicant/recipient group as long as all other eligibility conditions are met.

NOTE: Â If the individual applying is a LRR of a member of the applicant/recipient group, the deeming of income may make the entire group ineligible. (See Chapter 355, Deeming Income and Resources . ) Send an advance notice if the change affects the eligibility of any or all members of the applicant/recipient group . Determine their eligibility for other MA categories.

- If the applicant/recipient group is still eligible with the new applicant included, add the applicant effective the date of their application or the date the individual becomes eligible for NMP.

- Send a notice to applicant to let the applicant know of the decision.

Example: Â Mr. Stern receives PC for himself and his two children. His only income is his bi-weekly unemployment compensation check. On December 5, he reports that his wife (the mother of the two children) returned to the household. He fills out an application asking that she be added to the NMP budget. Â Mrs. Stern earns $50 a week as a housekeeper. The CAO determines the NMP category will now be PU. Â The CAO determines NMP eligibility using the total actual and expected income of both Mr. and Mrs. Stern for the calendar month of December. Mrs. Stern is qualified to receive the earned income incentive deduction. The applicant/recipient group, including Mrs. Stern, is eligible for NMP with her income counted. The CAO adds Mrs. Stern effective December 5 and changes the category to PU.

## 368.82 Removals from the Applicant/Recipient Group

A member must be removed from the applicant/recipient group when he or she leaves the household or no longer meets eligibility or category requirements. A member can also be removed when doing so would make the remaining members eligible for benefits.

When an individual is removed from an applicant/recipient group, the CAO must take the following actions:

- Determine NMP eligibility again for the other members, using the rules in Section 368.3, Continuing NMP , and Section 368.4, NMP Spend-Down .

- If the applicant/recipient group is ineligible with the individual removed, approve closure effective the day after the advance notice period ends.

Important: Â The CAO must consider eligibility for other MA categories.

- If the applicant/recipient group is eligible with the individual removed, continue NMP for the other members.

NOTE: Â If the member who is removed is a LRR who stays in the household, deem any countable income and resources of that individual. ( See Chapter 355, Deeming Income and Resources . )

- Send proper notice to let the applicant/recipient group know of the decision.
