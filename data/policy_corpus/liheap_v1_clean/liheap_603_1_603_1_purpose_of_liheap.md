# 603.1 Purpose of LIHEAP

The Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP) is a federal program that helps low-income households pay their heating bills.

LIHEAP offers help in three ways:

- A single Cash grant to help pay the costs of home heating.

- A Crisis grant to help with home-heating emergencies. Crisis grants:

- May be requested 24 hours per day, 7 days a week

- To a household that is without heat :

- Must be authorized within 18 hours after a complete application if there is a medical emergency or a life-threatening situation exists

- Must be authorized within 48 hours after a complete application if the applicant is out of fuel, has no heat because of a utility service shutoff

- To a household that is in danger o f being without heat :

- Must be authorized within 10 workdays if the household's Â fuel supply will run out within 15 days or before the household runs out , whichever is sooner .

- Must be authorized within 10 workdays of receiving a completed application of a household has received a shut-off notice or prior to the date of termination, whichever is sooner.

- Weatherization grants under the DHS/ DCED Emergency Services Program to fix emergency home-heating problems. DCED also provides non-emergency weatherization services to LIHEAP applicants and recipients. See Chapter 698, Weatherization .
