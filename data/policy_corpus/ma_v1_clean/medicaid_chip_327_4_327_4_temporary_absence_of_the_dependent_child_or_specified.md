# 327.4 Temporary Absence of the Dependent Child or Specified Relative

The specified relative is eligible for a MAGI-related category of MG 27 or Â TANF -related MA when the child and the specified relative are apart for a certain period of time (but not permanently) if:

55 Pa. Code Â§ 151.43(d)(2)&(3)

- The absence does not change the relative's role for being responsible for the care and control of the child.

- The specified relative will be responsible for the care and control of the child when they are back together.

Example: Mrs. M. is in the hospital to give birth. She continues to be responsible for the care and control of her other children. Mrs. M. is responsible for the children's needs and is in contact with them, even though her sister is temporarily watching them.

The child must continue to meet age requirements during the period of separation. If the dependent child lives at a school he or she is attending, the child and the specified relative remain eligible for MG 27 or TC as long as parental control is not given to school authorities.

A specified relative can get MG 27, SSI-related MA, or TANF-related MA for a minor child who is not in the home or is expected to be absent from the home for up to 180 days in a row. Â Â Â Â Â Â Â Â

The following are other examples of temporary absence:

- A child is temporarily living away from home because that makes the most sense for his or her education through the first 12 grades, undergraduate college or vocational training.

- A family is temporarily separated because of a fire, a flood, the sudden illness of a parent /caretaker or another reason.

- A child is making a court-ordered visit with a parent/ caretaker in another county or state.
