## 322.12 Noncooperation

Noncooperation means the refusal or failure of an applicant or recipient or that individualâs representative to work with CAO staff in trying to obtain required documentation or in explaining why it is not possible to provide proof of citizenship.

There are two kinds of noncooperation:

- An applicant or recipient refuses to provide citizenship documents.

- An applicant or recipient fails to cooperate by not returning requested items needed to obtain citizenship documentation (for example, an out-of-state birth certificate).

Exception: An applicant or recipient with a mental health or intellectual disability diagnosis may refuse to cooperate. These cases must be handled on a case by case basis. The CAO may need to consult with mental health/Â intellectual disability (MH/ID) caseworkers for help in obtaining needed documents.

An individual who refuses to cooperate with providing proof of citizenship or who fails to provide requested items needed to obtain proof of citizenship mayÂ have MA benefits closed after the individual is provided a reasonable opportunity period of 90 days, if the individual was otherwise eligible. Â A notice and information about appeal rights must be sent to the individual if the CAO denies or closes MA benefits.
