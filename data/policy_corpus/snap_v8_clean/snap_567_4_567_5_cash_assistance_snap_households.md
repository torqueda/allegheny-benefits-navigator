# 567.4 Cash Assistance/SNAP Households

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) households in which any member receives a cash assistance benefit are subject to prospective budgeting (PB) .

NOTE: Cash assistance benefits include Temporary Assistance for Needy Families ( TANF) ,Â Extended TANF (ETANF), and Transitional Cash Assistance (TCA) .

## 567.41 Cash Assistance Payments

The County Assistance Office ( CAO) must prospectively budget semimonthly cash assistance benefits to determine the SNAP benefit amount.

If the CAO is unable to determine the first cash assistance benefit amount or the receipt date, it must not count the payment. Â Â

The CAO must prospectively budget the cash assistance OTI. Example: Mrs. Miller and Mrs. Miller's two children receive cash assistance and SNAP benefits. Mrs. Miller receives unemployment compensation of $50 a week. On January 26, Mrs. Miller reports that Mr. Miller, an unemployed spouse moved back into the household. The CAO budgets the February benefit using the February cash assistance benefits for four persons (the OTI for February 2 through February 16 plus the February 17 semimonthly payment and the February unemployment compensation income). The CAO counts the portion of the cash OTI for the period January 29 through February 1 as a lump sum resource.

## 567.43 Retroactive Cash Assistance Payments

The CAO must use the portion of an initial cash assistance payment meant to cover a previous month's cycle as a lump sum payment and treat it as a resource in the month it is received. Â

The SNAP household may use the cash assistance benefit in its Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) account the day after authorization. The CAO must count the part of the cash assistance benefit that covers the current SNAP budget month and is reasonably expected to be received.

Example: A TANF /SNAP household is authorized on July 27. August payment dates are August 3 and August 18. The first cash assistance benefit for July 27 through August 17 is authorized and accessible to the household in the householdâs EBT account on July 28 (the day after authorization). The CAO counts only the payments for August 3 and August 18 for the August issuance. The prorated cash assistance benefit for the period July 27 through August 2 is a retroactive payment that must be counted as a resource in August.

## 567.44 Special Allowances

The CAO must not consider special items allowances or special allowances for supportive services issued under a cash assistance, medical assistance, or SNAP program as income for SNAP benefits. Special-items allowances and special allowances for supportive services include grants to decrease need, emergency grants, and child care allowances. The CAO must not count these payments as income, as they are reimbursements and are issued for a purpose other than basic living expenses. Â
