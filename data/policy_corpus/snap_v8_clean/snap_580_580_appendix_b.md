## County Payment Dates

Issuance of client benefits varies by county. The last digit of the seven-digit case record number determines the payment-day cycle number. Counties may have one, two, or ten payment-day cycles.

Examples:

- Beaver County is a one-payment-day cycle county. All recipients receive their benefits on payment cycle 3, regardless of the last digit of the case record number.

- Berks County is a two-payment-day cycle county. Recipients with case record numbers ending in 1 through 5 receive their benefits on payment-day cycle 4. Recipients with case record numbers ending in 6 through 0 receive their benefits on payment-day cycle 9.

- Lancaster County is a 10-payment-day cycle county. Recipients receive their benefits on the payment-day cycle that is the same as the last digit of the case record number.
