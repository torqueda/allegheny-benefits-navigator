# 511.9 Shelters for Battered Women

A shelter for battered persons is a public or private nonprofit residential facility for battered persons and their children. If a facility also serves other individuals, a part of the facility must be set aside on a long-term basis to serve only battered persons.

This section applies only to residents of shelters certified by Department of Human Services Â DHS . Shelters authorized by Food and Nutrition Services Â FNS to accept benefits are considered certified and do not require DHS certification.

## 511.91 Household Status

A temporary resident of a certified shelter is considered a separate SNAP household . The resident and their children may receive an additional benefit only once a month as a separate household if they are still included in the abuser's SNAP household .

- If the person is included in the abuser's household , the CAO must take prompt action to remove their children from the former household's budget. (See Chapter 570 .)

- A person and their Â children temporarily living in places other than a certified shelter may not receive an additional benefit as a separate household .

## 511.93 Certification Periods

The certification period for a person in a shelter is usually one month (or two if certified after the 15th of the month) because residentsâ stays usually are brief.

## 511.94 Nondisclosure of Shelter Address

Important: Since the safety of shelter residents may be at risk the CAO must not disclose the address or location of a shelter except to administrators of SNAP or other federal assistance programs.

## 511.95 Certified Shelters

If authorized by FNS, a shelter may accept SNAP benefits for meals provided to the resident and use the benefits to buy food from wholesalers.

If not authorized by FNS, a shelter may be an AR and use SNAP benefits at retail stores to buy food for prepared meals served to residents.
