# 555.2 Disqualification for Intentional Program Violation

The Office of Inspector General (OIG) Bureau of Fraud Prevention and Prosecution will update the Disqualified Recipient System (DQRS) for a person who is to be disqualified for an intentional program violation . The terms of the disqualification must be entered by the County Assistance Office (CAO) in the automated system. Â Â Â

The CAO must not count the following disqualified persons when determining the household size, resource limit, benefit level, or the gross and net income eligibility limits: Â

- A fleeing felon;

- A probation or parole violator being actively sought (see Chapter 503 Appendix B for more details);Â

- An intentional program violator; or

- A work requirement non-compliant individual

## 555.23 Deductions

The CAO must include allowable deductions (standard, earned income, medical, dependent care, child support, and excess shelter) for all household members, including the ineligible member , in the computation for the household.
