# Priority Heuristic

Priority order is the order in which the navigator suggests a user review recommended programs first. It is based mainly on how likely the household appears to match each program and secondarily on immediate hardship signals such as food need, health coverage need, or heating need. It is a triage heuristic for follow-up, not an official statement of legal urgency, benefit amount, or application difficulty.

## What Influences Ranking

- likely applicability for the household
- program profile fit based on program-specific signals and thresholds
- immediate hardship or urgency signals such as food insecurity, no health coverage, pregnancy-related coverage need, or heating crisis
- practical next-step value, meaning which recommendation appears most actionable to review first
- missing or contradictory evidence, which should lower certainty and increase caveats or human follow-up rather than create overconfident ranking

## How Evaluation Uses Priority

Evaluation treats program applicability separately from priority order.

- applicability checks ask whether the right programs were recommended or suppressed
- priority checks are narrower and directional, such as whether the top recommendation falls within an acceptable set for the case
- a PASS result does not mean every priority limitation is solved; it means the current evaluator accepted the observed behavior under the documented prototype rules
